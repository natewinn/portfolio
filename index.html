About Me

At the age of thirty, I set out to achieve a goal—to make an even greater impact with the work I do. To do this, I knew I must learn the language of the future. I was so serious about it that I left my job as a director at my church, North Valley Baptist Church, to complete an immersive three-month Ruby on Rails bootcamp at The Iron Yard. Now, I'm ready to roll up my sleeves and contribute my love for design, project management, problem solving, and now coding to an incredible team.

I live in the heart of the Silicon Valley and enjoy exploring Northern California with my wife Jo and my little boy Owen. Away from work, you'll find me aeropressing coffee, running, reading on my kindle, or jotting my thoughts online.

Mobber Mockup

A challenge to create pixel-perfect markup of a fictitious flash-mob website. I translated this original screenshot into functioning HTML/CSS markup. I made use of the 960 Grid System to give me a hand with the layout and Google Fonts to help match up the type faces as close as possible.

EventFull

A simple photo sharing app built with Ruby on Rails. Users can easily upload their own photos using the CarrierWave gem, add unique comments, and apply global tags to each photo. Haml was used for the markup, Foundation for the framework, and PostgreSQL for the database.

Schedule Tune

Piano tuners can also keep their customer databases in tune. Built on RoR, Schedule Tune uses Amazon S3 for photo uploads of customer's pianos, Geocoder and Google Maps API for easy, one-click directions to a customer's residence, and Full Calendar to display customer appointments with JSON.
